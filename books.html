This is not a story about something extraordinary happening. No dramatic turning point. No single moment where everything changes. No version of love that fixes a life overnight. This is a story about what it feels like to exist quietly in a crowded world. About waking up every day surrounded by people and still feeling slightly out of place. About scrolling through a phone full of names and realizing none of them really know you. About laughing at the right moments, saying the safe things, and carrying thoughts you never quite say out loud. It’s about being seventeen and expected to know where you’re going, when most days you’re still figuring out where you stand. This book doesn’t move fast. It doesn’t chase drama. It pays attention instead — to silence, to small moments, and to what we don’t say.
